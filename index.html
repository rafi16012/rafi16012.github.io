01 — About

A little about me

I grew up in Bangladesh, a country shaped by its rivers, its monsoons, and the quiet, ongoing negotiation between people and water. That relationship pulled me toward Water Resources Engineering long before I knew exactly what the field involved. At BUET, I found a discipline that married rigorous problem-solving with questions I genuinely cared about: how do we live well alongside rivers, and how do we engineer for a climate that no longer behaves like the one our textbooks were written for?

I currently work as a Research Assistant in BUET's Geotechnical Engineering Department, which has been an unexpected and rewarding turn. Sitting at the edge of water and soil mechanics has shown me how interconnected these fields really are — from riverbank stability and embankment failure to subsidence and groundwater dynamics. My broader interests include flood and river management, environmental engineering, climate change adaptation, and sustainable development. Outside of work, I enjoy listening to music, cooking and talking about innovation. I'm preparing for graduate studies and looking for a research home where I can contribute meaningfully to climate-resilient water engineering —ideally in collaboration with people who care as much about the problem as the solution.
