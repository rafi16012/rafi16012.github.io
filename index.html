01 — About

A little about me

I grew up in Bangladesh, surrounded by rivers and monsoons. At BUET, I discovered my passion for Water Resources Engineering, where I now work as a Research Assistant focusing on flood management, climate adaptation, and sustainable development.

Outside work, I love music, cooking, and exploring innovation, as I prepare for my next step in climate-resilient water engineering.

We don't engineer against water — we engineer in conversation with it. The rivers were here first, and they'll outlast whatever we build. The work is in listening well.

— My research philosophy

Modeling & Simulation

I've built HEC-HMS models for the lower Teesta basin to forecast 48-hour flood peaks, and I'm comfortable moving between 1D river hydraulics in HEC-RAS and urban drainage in SWMM. The fun part is calibration — translating messy field data into a model that actually behaves like the real system.

GIS & Remote Sensing

Spatial analysis & earth observation
ArcGIS / QGIS Google Earth Engine SNAP Remote Sensing
+ tap to learn more
From classifying Dhaka's land-use change in Google Earth Engine to processing Sentinel data in SNAP, I lean on remote sensing whenever a problem needs a view from above. GIS is the connective tissue — turning satellite pixels and survey points into maps that tell a story.
